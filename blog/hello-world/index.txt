f:T70d,<p>For years, my public-facing work has lived in the pages of IEEE journals and conference proceedings. While I deeply value the rigor of peer review, I have come to realize that the most impactful ideas often start as informal conversations — the kind that happen in hallways after a talk, or in a Slack thread with a colleague.</p>
<p>This blog is my attempt to bring those conversations into the open.</p>
<h2>What to Expect</h2>
<p>I plan to write about the topics that occupy my daily thinking:</p>
<p><strong>Smart Glass &#x26; Multi-Modal AI</strong> — As an engineer at Meta working on the next generation of smart glass systems, I sit at the intersection of hardware constraints and AI ambition. I will share insights on the challenges and breakthroughs in building AI systems that must operate in the real world, on your face, in real time.</p>
<p><strong>Academic Life &#x26; Industry Practice</strong> — As an IEEE Associate Editor and a practicing engineer, I have a unique vantage point on how academic research translates (or fails to translate) into production systems. I want to explore this gap honestly.</p>
<p><strong>Engineering Leadership</strong> — Building great systems requires more than great algorithms. I will write about the organizational and leadership challenges of shipping AI products at scale.</p>
<h2>Why Now?</h2>
<p>The field of AI is moving at an unprecedented pace. The gap between what is published in papers and what is deployed in products is shrinking rapidly. I believe that engineers and researchers who can articulate their thinking clearly — not just in formal publications, but in accessible, timely writing — will have an outsized impact on the direction of the field.</p>
<p>This is my contribution to that conversation. Welcome aboard.</p>
